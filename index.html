A bit about me: These days, I'm both a high school STEM teacher and a part-time researcher. I graduated from MIT with a B.S. in Physics in 2018, and went on to receive an M.Ed. at a new competency-based program in Cambridge. I've been working in classrooms, informal learning spaces & educational summer programs since 2014.

I am interested in metacognition and ultimately helping learners to both achieve their goals & become their best selves. I began my higher education believing I would go into astrophysics, but officially transitioned from the physical sciences to secondary education in my undergraduate senior year. A generalist at heart, I have studied linguistics, earth sciences, astronomy, literature, biology, and behavioral sciences; that said, my enduring passion has been & continues to be learning about learning.

Laughter and tears are both responses to frustration and exhaustion. I myself prefer to laugh, since there is less cleaning do to do afterward.
